On Learn, Unlearn, Relearn — and why most people only do one of them

The Toffler quote gets cited constantly in L&D circles. The actual practice of all three is much harder and much more specific than the slogan suggests. Here's what it looks like when you actually try.

"The illiterate of the 21st century will not be those who cannot read and write, but those who cannot learn, unlearn, and relearn." — Alvin Toffler

Everyone cites this quote. It shows up in conference keynotes, L&D onboarding decks, and LinkedIn posts by people who have definitely not thought too hard about what the middle word actually requires. Almost nobody does all three.

Most of us are pretty good at learning. Passably good at relearning. And almost universally terrible at unlearning — not because we're not trying, but because we don't fully understand what it actually asks of us.
