About

Hi, I'm Zulsyika.

Learning designer, systems builder, professional yapper. Based in Jakarta, curious about everything.

I'm a learning designer based in Indonesia with a Bachelor's degree in Educational Technology and a Master's degree in Learning Technology and Experience Design. I have two years of experience developing tools and resources for enterprise training at PT Nindya Karya, as well as experience in higher education at the NYU Learning and Teaching Nexus.

My job is to design, develop, utilize, and evaluate knowledge and learning experiences, both offline and online, across platforms and contexts, for learners and educators alike. My aim is to create learner-centered, effective, and adaptive learning environments for all.

Currently, I'm leading digital transformation at YPKBI, building the tools and systems that support students, educators, and administrators across a network of schools. The work spans instructional design, full-stack web development, data systems, and everything in between. I like it when a role doesn't have clean edges.

Interests

"Learn, Unlearn, Relearn."

I hold those three concepts close to my heart because of how impactful they are. In the spirit of lifelong learning, these three are basically the cheat sheet. By continuously acquiring new knowledge, letting go of obsolete knowledge and mindsets, and updating understanding with new perspective, we're able to adapt, innovate, and collaborate well. It is a full circle of learning in this rapidly changing world!

With that, I always try to approach things with a learner's or novice's mindset, in the hope of being teachable and absorbing as much knowledge and skills as possible.

I am also a professional yapper, especially when it comes to things I'm passionate about. But lately, I'm trying to regulate myself and listen more, so if you catch me being quiet, I'm probably in my contemplation™ mode.

Custom-built on Vercel. More than a portfolio now, also a blog (megaZN). The name that finally felt right.

I like the name theznprojects because it has my initials, "ZN", but the whole name can also be pronounced as "design projects." I think that's pretty fun. The logo is a negative space treatment of my initials, where both the words "The" and "Projects" sit on the letter Z.

This version is custom-built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, hosted on Vercel. No frameworks, no bloat, just the things the site actually needs.
